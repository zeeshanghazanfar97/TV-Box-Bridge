# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People controlling a cable TV box from a browser on the same LAN. They are likely using a laptop, tablet, or phone near a TV, often from a couch or desk, and need the interface to work quickly without hunting through oversized controls.

## Product Purpose

TV Box Bridge turns an HDMI capture feed and an IR transmitter into a browser-based TV control surface. Success means the stream is easy to start, the hardware status is visible, and common remote actions are fast while the complete physical remote remains available.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, capable, practical. The interface should feel like reliable home-lab equipment: direct, compact, and calm under imperfect hardware conditions.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-page composition, decorative dashboards, oversized remote controls, novelty skeuomorphism, and colorful controls that make repeated TV navigation slower.

## Design Principles

- Keep the live picture dominant.
- Make the default remote small enough to leave the stream readable.
- Preserve every physical remote key, but hide infrequent controls behind a deliberate reveal.
- Prefer hardware-truthful status over optimistic labels.
- Use familiar remote-control affordances so users can operate by memory.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target keyboard and pointer usability, visible focus states, touch-friendly controls, and reduced reliance on color-only state. Motion should be short and tied to feedback or reveal.
